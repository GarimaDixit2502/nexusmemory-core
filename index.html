NexusMemory Core

A Three-Tier Cognitive Architecture for Asynchronous Vector and Graph-Driven LLM Memory.

Large Language Models (LLMs) are incredibly smart, but they suffer from short-term memory limits—they forget past context, get confused during long conversations, and hallucinate facts. NexusMemory Core acts as an external, permanent brain for AI models. It mimics human biology by organizing information into three distinct layers, ensuring your AI applications remember every document, conversation, and relationship perfectly over time.

How It Works: The 3-Layer Brain

Instead of dumping data into a basic flat database, NexusMemory Core processes and filters information through a dynamic pipeline:

1

Short-Term Buffer (Immediate Awareness)

Captures raw, incoming streams of data or user queries instantly. It handles real-time information processing through a lightning-fast data queue.

2

Mid-Term Episodic Memory (Contextual Vector Space)

Converts your data into multi-dimensional vectors. When you ask a question, a custom scoring system ranks past events by balancing how relevant they are, how recently you mentioned them, and how often they are accessed.

3

Long-Term Semantic Graph (The Knowledge Network)

An asynchronous background worker automatically distills aging memories into an interconnected knowledge web. It maps out how different entities and ideas relate to one another, unlocking complex reasoning capabilities for your AI.

The Result

When an AI app queries NexusMemory Core, it receives a perfectly synthesized context block pulled from across all three layers in milliseconds. Your AI stays deeply grounded, hyper-accurate, and entirely immune to forgetfulness.
